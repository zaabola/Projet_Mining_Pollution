How It Works

This module uses the Best UNet Ghada Model (a ResNet34-based U-Net architecture) trained specifically to segment and identify mining areas from satellite imagery.

  1. Image Processing: The uploaded satellite image is normalized and processed by the neural network.
  2. Detection: The model generates a binary mask where positive pixels (red) indicate the presence of a mining area.
  3. Soil Health Correlation: Environmental studies show that intensive mining severely impacts surrounding soil quality. If the model detects a significant mining area in the image, the system automatically flags the Soil Health as BAD. Otherwise, it is considered GOOD.
Note: Mining activities can lead to soil compaction, contamination by heavy metals, and loss of biodiversity.
